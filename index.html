Course Description

This course is for anyone who is working in nutritional sciences or data related to nutrition. In this course, we will discuss the principles and calculations involved in nutrition. The following quantitative nutrition concepts will be covered in the course:

  • - Habitual nutrient intake and its estimation methods.
  • - Dietary nutrient requirement and its determination.
  • - The concept of nutrient inadequacy and its measures.
  • - Computation of population level nutrient gaps to be met with fortification or supplementation.
  • - Growth metrices and their application.

  • He is an Associate Professor in the Department of Biostatistics with over 20 years of experience in teaching and research.   • Dr. Anura V Kurpad - Professor, Dept. of Physiology, St. John’s Medical College (SJMC)
  • Dr. Anura Vishwanath Kurpad, MBBS, MD, DNB, PhD, is a renowned professor and Head of Physiology at St. John’s Medical College, Bangalore, and leads the IAEA Collaborating Centre at the same institution. His research focuses on nutrient metabolism, including energy, protein, amino acids, iron, and vitamin A, with a special interest in undernutrition, obesity, and the human nutrition-agriculture interface. Dr. Kurpad has authored over 360 scientific papers, 321 of which are indexed in PubMed, and has co-authored multiple books, including Guyton’s Textbook of Physiology (Asian editions) and Nutritional Management of Obesity and Diabetes. He is among one of the World's Top 2% Scientists in 2024, listed by Stanford University and also included in the carrer-long list for 2024. He has chaired several prominent national scientific committees, including the ICMR Nutrition Division, and has served as a consultant for the IAEA. He has been recognized with numerous awards, such as the Sir CV Raman Young Scientist Award and the Asia-Pacific Clinical Nutrition Society Award. Additionally, Dr. Kurpad is a Fellow of the International Union of Nutritional Sciences and the National Academy of Medical Sciences, India. His significant contributions to both academia and public health nutrition make him a leading figure in the field of human physiology and nutrition.
